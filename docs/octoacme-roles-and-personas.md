# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape the user experience strategy and ensure that product decisions are grounded in user needs. They collaborate closely with Product Managers and Developers to deliver intuitive, accessible, and well-validated interfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Define user experience strategy aligned with product goals
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes for new features
- Lead usability testing and synthesize findings into actionable recommendations
- Maintain a design system and ensure visual/interaction consistency
- Collaborate with Product Managers on requirements and with Developers on implementation feasibility

### Goals
- Deliver design assets that enable fast, confident implementation
- Reduce rework through early usability validation
- Advocate for the end user in product and engineering decisions

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with Developers and Product Managers
- Usability test readouts with stakeholders
- Design handoff documentation and annotated specs

### Interaction with Other Roles
| Role | Interaction |
|---|---|
| Product Manager | Collaborates on problem framing, feature requirements, and success metrics |
| Developers | Provides design specs and answers implementation questions |
| QA Engineer | Reviews usability and visual consistency during testing |
| Project Manager | Communicates design timelines and dependencies |

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers maintain process and project documentation to ensure that information is accurate, accessible, and current. They drive onboarding clarity and manage the quality of all written communication produced by the team.

### Responsibilities
- Author and maintain process documents, runbooks, and user guides
- Draft and publish release notes for each release cycle
- Collaborate with Developers and Product Managers to capture technical content accurately
- Manage onboarding documentation for new team members
- Establish and enforce documentation standards across the project

### Goals
- Ensure all team members and stakeholders have the information they need, when they need it
- Reduce onboarding time for new contributors
- Produce release notes and communication that are clear and consistent

### Typical Communication
- Document review cycles with subject matter experts (Developers, PMs)
- Handoff check-ins at the end of each release cycle
- Onboarding walkthroughs and documentation feedback sessions

### Interaction with Other Roles
| Role | Interaction |
|---|---|
| Developers | Reviews technical content for accuracy; captures API and implementation details |
| Product Manager | Aligns on feature summaries and user-facing release communication |
| Project Manager | Coordinates documentation milestones with release timelines |
| QA Engineer | Reviews documentation for testability and accuracy before release |

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers design and execute test strategies that ensure product quality and validate acceptance criteria. They build automated and manual test suites, triage defects, and serve as the quality gate before releases.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain the overall test strategy and test plan
- Build and maintain automated test suites (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Execute manual and exploratory testing for feature acceptance
- Triage, document, and track defects through to resolution
- Define and enforce the Definition of Done as it relates to quality

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase test coverage and automation to reduce manual regression effort
- Provide clear, timely quality signals to the delivery team

### Typical Communication
- Daily participation in standups to surface quality risks and blockers
- Defect reports and test result summaries shared with Developers and PMs
- Pre-release quality sign-off communicated to Project Manager and stakeholders

### Interaction with Other Roles
| Role | Interaction |
|---|---|
| Developers | Partners closely during development to clarify acceptance criteria and validate fixes |
| Product Manager | Reviews acceptance criteria and confirms feature readiness |
| Project Manager | Reports quality status and surfaces risks to release timelines |
| Scrum Master | Raises process impediments that affect quality workflows |

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove blockers, and help the team maintain a healthy and productive delivery rhythm. They enforce process standards and coach team members in agile best practices.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove impediments that block the team's progress
- Protect the team from scope creep and unplanned interruptions
- Coach team members in agile principles and continuous improvement
- Maintain team metrics (velocity, cycle time) and surface trends to leadership

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver at a predictable and sustainable pace
- Continuously improve team processes through retrospective action items
- Reduce friction and blockers before they impact delivery

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies facilitation
- Retrospective summaries and action item tracking
- Blockers and dependency escalations to Project Manager and stakeholders

### Interaction with Other Roles
| Role | Interaction |
|---|---|
| Project Manager | Aligns on delivery status, escalations, and risk mitigation |
| Product Manager | Collaborates on backlog grooming cadence and sprint scope |
| Developers | Coaches on agile practices; surfaces and removes blockers |
| QA Engineer | Ensures quality activities are factored into sprint capacity and DoD |

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and document requirements, map business processes, and translate stakeholder needs into precise technical language for the delivery team. They serve as the bridge between business stakeholders and the engineering team.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate requirements with stakeholders
- Map current and future-state business processes
- Translate business requirements into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Validate delivered features against original requirements
- Facilitate stakeholder workshops and requirement reviews

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, unambiguous, and agreed upon before development begins
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or missing requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story reviews with Product Manager and Developers
- Post-delivery validation sessions with stakeholders

### Interaction with Other Roles
| Role | Interaction |
|---|---|
| Product Manager | Collaborates on backlog refinement and requirement prioritization |
| Developers | Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria during implementation |
| Project Manager | Provides requirements status and flags scope risks |
| Scrum Master | Supports backlog refinement and story readiness ceremonies |
